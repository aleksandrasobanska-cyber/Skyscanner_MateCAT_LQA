# Skyscanner Japanese (JA-JP) – LQA Calibration & Style Guide

## I. CRITICAL LQA CALIBRATION (READ FIRST)
**You must prioritize Functional Integrity and Logic over Stylistic Preference.** Use the following hierarchy to determine errors:

### 1. LOGIC & MATH (Highest Severity - ALWAYS FLAG)
- **Directional Logic (Critical):** Check for inverted meanings in variables.
  - *Example:* "From {City}" (Origin) vs. "To {City}" (Destination). Translating "From Tokyo" as "To Tokyo" (東京行き) is a **Major** error.
- **Logic Inversion:** Watch for inverted conditions.
  - *Example:* "Cancel up to 24h before" ≠ "Cancel after 24h" (予約後).
  - *Example:* "One day later" (+1 Day) ≠ "One day late" (Delay).
- **Dynamic Price Integrity:** Do NOT accept the addition of intensifiers like "only" or "just" next to a dynamic `{{price}}` variable unless present in the source.
- **UI Functionality:** Ensure translation matches user state.
  - *Example:* "Sign up" (Newsletter) ≠ "登録" (Register Account) if logged in. Use "購読".
  - *Example:* "Save" (Action) ≠ "Check" (Action) unless functionality is identical.

### 2. TERMINOLOGY & GLOSSARY (Strict Enforcement)
- **Adherence:** If `tb_matches` provides a term, you **MUST** flag deviations.
- **Katakana Compounds:** Use a Nakaguro (・) for compound proper nouns (e.g., `シアトル・タコマ`, `トロント・ピアソン`). Do NOT use spaces or direct concatenation.
- **Product Names:** "Explore Everywhere" = "すべての場所から探す".
- **Technical Terms:**
  - **AdTech:** "Header Bidding" = "Header Bidding" (Keep English) or "ヘッダービディング".
  - **Legal:** "Opt-out" = "オプトアウト" or "無効化".

### 3. ANTI-FALSE POSITIVE RULES (Do NOT Flag)
**Do NOT flag the following scenarios. These are valid variations, not errors:**
- **Particle Nuance:** Do NOT flag subtle particle choices unless they create a grammatical error.
  - *Example:* `ホテルの滞在` (Hotel's stay) vs `ホテルでの滞在` (Stay at hotel). Both are acceptable noun phrases in UI.
- **Politeness Level (Desu/Masu):** Only flag inconsistencies (mixing Plain and Polite forms in the *same* list/screen). Do not flag "Polite" forms as "Bureaucratic" unless the brand guide explicitly demands "Plain" style for that specific component.
- **Synonyms:** "Search" = `検索` or `探す`. Both are acceptable.

## II. PRODUCT & WRITING GUIDELINES

### Tone & Style
- **Goal:** Natural, conversational Japanese; use friendly 丁寧語 (です/ます).
- **Avoid:** Overly stiff/translation-ese (Literal translation of "You" like `あなた`).
- **Register:**
  - *Standard:* Use Polite form (Desu/Masu) for body copy and messages.
  - *UI Labels:* Use Noun phrases or Suru-verbs (e.g., `検索`, `保存する`).

### Formatting & Conventions
- **Punctuation:** Use full-width punctuation (`。`, `、`). No spaces between Japanese characters.
- **Numbers:** Half-width Arabic numerals (`1, 2, 3`). Use commas for thousands (`1,000`).
- **Date/Time:** `2025年11月`, `24時間`.
- **Katakana:** Avoid excessive Katakana if a natural Japanese term exists.

### Content Types
- **UI/CTAs:** Concise, action-oriented. `料金を表示`, `直行便を表示`.
- **Imperatives:** Translate "Find X" as "Xを見つける" or "Xを探す" (Volitional/Active) rather than imperative commands.

## III. ERROR REPORTING GUIDE (Impact-Driven)

- **Critical/Major Error:**
  - Logic inversion (20% off vs 80% price).
  - Wrong Preposition on Variables (From/To).
  - Functional Mismatch (Register vs Subscribe).
  - Wrong Technical Term (AdTech).
  - Broken/Missing Placeholders.
  - Inconsistent Katakana spacing in Proper Nouns (Missing Nakaguro).
- **Minor Error:**
  - Punctuation typos (Double comma).
  - Mixed register in lists (Plain/Polite mix).
  - Trailing punctuation in UI labels.
- **False Positive (Do Not Report):**
  - "Better" synonyms.
  - `の` vs `での` (unless meaning is lost).
  - Stylistic rephrasing that doesn't change meaning.
