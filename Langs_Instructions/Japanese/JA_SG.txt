Skyscanner Japanese LQA Model Instructions
1. Skyscanner’s Way of Writing
1.1. Mission Reflection in Writing
 • Emphasize simplicity and ease of use: “Planning and booking your trip should be simple, not a chore.”
 • Highlight straightforward and speedy app/website: “We're making sure our app and website are super straightforward and speedy.”
 • Mention multilingual availability: “Our app and website are available in more than 30 languages.”
 • Reflect sustainability:
  - Use phrases like “A 'Greener choice' label which highlights the flights that emit less CO2.”
  - Mention sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) and partnerships: “We buy sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) to help offset any travel we do here at Skyscanner.” “We’re working with the Duke of Sussex, and industry leaders, to form Travalyst.”
1.2. Tone of Voice (ToV)
 • Four core characteristics:
  - Helpful: Provide clear, useful information; focus on user benefit.
  - Joyful: Convey excitement about travel; use accessible humor and warmth.
  - Straightforward: Use clear, everyday language; avoid jargon and business speak.
  - Bold: Be inspiring, empowering, and not afraid to voice opinions.
 • Apply ToV principles according to content type, purpose, and intended message.
 • ToV philosophy:
  - Helpful appeals to rationality (head).
  - Joyful appeals to emotion (heart).
  - Straightforward appeals to instinct (gut).
  - Bold inspires action (feet).
2. General Writing & Translation Guidelines
2.1. Good Writing Principles
 • Always put the traveller first.
 • Avoid excessive adjectives.
 • Ensure every sentence is helpful and user-focused.
 • Always consider the user’s perspective.
2.2. Writing & Translation Guidelines
 • Make translations conversational and natural, not stiff or overly formal.
 • Ensure clarity and correctness; use well-known terms.
 • Translate meaning, not just words.
 • Keep language simple and sentences short.
 • Use active voice, especially in headlines and CTAs.
 • Address the reader directly; use informal singular “you” (あなた) for general content, お客様 for formal contexts.
 • Be specific and provide actionable details.
 • Write as if talking to a friend; ensure naturalness.
 • Make it personal and use real experiences.
 • Be interesting and engaging.
 • Be honest and transparent about pros and cons.
 • Double-check all factual details (spellings, times, locations).
 • Use exclamation marks sparingly.
3. Language-Specific Translation Guidelines
3.1. Punctuation & Spacing
 • Pay attention to HTML tags; do not insert spaces between tags and text.
 • Use 「」for button/section names when not clear in context (e.g., 「トリップス」「発見」).
3.3. Addressing Users – YOU
 • Use あなた for marketing/blog/general promotion.
 • Use お客様 for formal contexts (e.g., emails to specific users).
 • Avoid excessive use of あなた to prevent unnaturalness.
3.4. Active Voice
 • Avoid passive voice unless necessary (e.g., terms and conditions, negative events).
 • Do not mix active and passive voice in the same context.
 • Example:
  - Good: お客さまのデータを保存しました。
  - Bad: お客さまのデータが保存されました。
3.5. Formats (Numbers, Dates, Currencies, etc.)
3.5.1. Avoiding Misunderstanding
 • Avoid ambiguous expressions, especially in booking/contract contexts.
 • Example:
  - Bad: より多くのホテルが無料キャンセルを提供しています。今予約しても、後ほどキャンセル可能です。
  - Good: 多くのホテルで無料キャンセルが可能です。そうしたホテルでは、今予約してあとでキャンセルすることもできます。
3.5.2. Headlines
 • For headlines, do not use ですます調; use 体言止め.
 • Examples:
  - ホテルの価格をアプリでチェック
  - 安心して旅の計画を
  - ホテルの清潔度もすぐわかる
3.5.3. Numbers, Dates, and Time
 • Use 1-byte numbers; do not use 2-byte numbers.
 • Use Arabic numerals for countable items (e.g., 1回目、第3回、2名様).
 • Use 漢数字 for idiomatic expressions (e.g., 万が一, 一人ひとり).
 • Use commas for numbers (e.g., 1,000名様), but not for years (e.g., 2020年4月9日).
 • Date format: YYYY年MM月DD日 (preferred), or YYYY/MM/DD if character limit.
 • Time: Use 12-hour clock with 午前/午後HH時MM分 unless otherwise specified.
 • Examples:
  - 11:30 AM → 11:30（半角コロン）, 午前11時30分
  - 7:30 PM → 19:30, 午後7時30分
  - 12:00 AM → 24:00, 深夜0時 (do not use 午前零時)
3.5.4. Currencies
 • Do not localize currency unless requested.
 • Use: 100ポンド, 100ドル, 100ユーロ
3.5.5. Units of Measure
 • Convert all measurements to metric (m, km, ㎡, ヘクタール).
 • If both imperial and metric are shown, do not convert; use マイル for miles, km for kilometers.
 • Rounding: round down if <.5, round up if ≥.5.
 • Examples:
  - 3.6 miles = 5.8km
  - 1,200 sq. feet = 112㎡
 • Do not convert if referring to airline miles.
3.5.6. Countries
 • Use official country names in Japanese; consult the Ministry of Foreign Affairs for exceptions.
3.5.7. URL Translation
 • No spaces; use hyphens only.
 • All lowercase.
 • No extended/special characters.
 • Use only Latin characters; for Japanese, leave URLs in English.
 • If URLs are split, reverse order if established translation requires.
 • Example: city-breaks → escapades-urbanes (for CA).
3.6. Linguistic Sequence
3.6.1. Transliteration
 • Transliterate people’s names and most foreign place names.
 • Use correct Japanese terms for Japanese/Chinese names; for Korean, use transliteration.
 • If unsure of Japanese name in kanji, use katakana.
 • Skyscanner leadership names remain in English.
 • Do not translate/transliterate names with ™ or ®, or major app/brand/company names (Google, Facebook, etc.).
 • Use 常用漢字 only; some words should be in hiragana (e.g., そろえる, おすすめ, ぜひ, ください).
 • Avoid excessive katakana; prefer Japanese terms.
3.6.2. Organization Names at National Level
 • Google for official Japanese names; if not found, keep in English.
3.6.3. NGO, Company, Association Names
 • Google for official Japanese names; if not found, keep in English.
4. Translation Guidelines per Content Type
4.1. User Interface (UI)
 • Check website/app for term consistency.
 • Keep translation length similar to source.
 • Use current web lingo (link, continue, go, next, accept, click here, search).
 • Write concise, attention-grabbing, easy-to-read sentences with crucial info.
4.6. Legal, Policies, Terms & Conditions
 • Be clear and concise; avoid bureaucratic language.
 • Address Skyscanner as スカイスキャナー or 当社 as appropriate.
 • Address user as お客様.
 • Use ですます調.

6.1. Alternative Words & Expressions
 • Use preferred alternatives to maintain helpful, straightforward, joyful, and bold tone.
 • Examples:
  - accommodation → hotels, hostels, apartments, room, where you’re staying
  - acquire → get
  - additional → extra, more
  - administration → admin
  - adverse → bad, harmful, dangerous
  - airplane → aeroplane (UK), airplane (US), plane
  - allow → let
  - assist → help
  - clarify → tell you about, explain, make clear
  - click → use descriptive alternatives (e.g., Visit hotels page)
  - contact us → use get in touch, give us a call, email us, write to us at <email> (except in headlines/CTAs)
  - convenient → good, easy, useful
  - customer → you, your traveller
  - discuss → chat, talk
  - ensure → make sure
  - feedback → tell us your thoughts, let us know what you think
  - holiday → trip, holiday, break, getaway
  - inform → tell, talk
  - obtain → get
  - purchase → buy
  - receive → get
  - review → check, look into, see, view, read, watch
  - suitable → right, OK, relevant, good, useful
  - user → traveller, you, your
  - utilise → use
  - whilst → while
7. Visual Examples
 • Use imperative, concise forms for CTAs and headlines:
  - Like this: “Discover our map”
  - Not like this: “Discovering our map”
 • Test naturalness by reading copy out loud.
 • Use correct, natural word choices in context (e.g., “Deine Daten. Deine Entscheidung.” not “Deine Daten. Deine Wahl.”).
