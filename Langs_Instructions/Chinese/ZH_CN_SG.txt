Skyscanner Simplified Chinese LQA Model Instructions
1. Skyscanner’s Tone of Voice (ToV)
 • Familiarize yourself with Skyscanner’s Tone of Voice as outlined in the referenced document.
 • Ensure consistency with existing translations and the established ToV.
2. General Writing & Translation Guidelines
2.1. Essence of the Message
 • Convey the message and tone of the original text in natural, fluent Simplified Chinese.
 • Avoid word-for-word translation; do not imitate the source structure if it sounds unnatural.
 • Do not add or remove facts, but feel free to restructure sentences for naturalness.
 • Shorten or break up long sentences where appropriate.
 • Use active verbs to describe processes.
2.2. Vocabulary and Grammar
 • Use simple, clear, and concise language.
 • Avoid formality, bureaucracy, and complexity.
 • Prioritize clarity and brevity, especially for web and mobile content.
 • Be friendly, approachable, and trustworthy.
 • Always prioritize clarity above cleverness.
2.3. Local Sayings and Terms
 • Use natural, engaging local sayings and terms to make content approachable.
 • Transcreate content to adapt messages and emotions to the local market and culture.
 • Avoid region-specific or provincial terms that may exclude parts of the audience.
 • Avoid excessive slang or colloquialisms; ensure nothing is vulgar or offensive.
2.4. Human Tone
 • Avoid robotic or unnatural language.
 • Use plain, honest, and jargon-free language.
2.5. Examples (Chinese)
 • Source: {place_name}, or elsewhere?
  - ✔ 除了{place_name}，还能去哪里？
  - ✖ 去{place_name}或其他地方？
 • Source: Still dreaming of {place_name}?
  - ✔ 还想去{place_name}吗？
  - ✖ 还在幻想{place_name}？
2.6. Stress Test
 • Ensure writing is understandable and clear to a broad audience.
 • Read translations out loud to check for naturalness and clarity.
2.7. Summary
 • Avoid literal translations; focus on transcreation.
 • Read translations out loud for naturalness.
 • Use simple, clear, and active sentences; avoid complex structures.
 • Maintain consistency with Skyscanner’s ToV and existing translations.
3. Language-Specific Translation Guidelines
3.1. Brand Name
 • Do NOT localize "Skyscanner"; always keep it in English.
3.2. Punctuation & Spaces
 • Use full-width (全角) punctuation for all marks, including commas, exclamation marks, and brackets（括号）.
 • Use squared quotation marks: 「」.
 • Do NOT add extra spaces between Chinese characters and numbers or English letters.
3.3. Provincial Terms
 • Avoid provincial terms, including those used in Hong Kong, Macau, or Taiwan but not in Mainland China.
 • Example:
  - Taxi: ✔ 出租车; ✖ 计程车/的士
3.4. Positive / Negative Implications
 • Use positive, neutral, or appropriate terms.
 • Examples:
  - ✔ 这里充满欢乐氛围; ✖ 这里充斥着欢乐氛围
  - ✔ 当地政府部门/地方政府; ✖ 地方当局
3.5. Addressing Users (“you”)
 • Translate “you” as “您”.
 • For plural, use “你们”.
3.6. Formats (Numbers, Dates, Currencies, etc.)
3.6.1. Dates and Time
 • Do NOT use Chinese characters for numbers in dates/times.
  - Yes: 2016年4月15日／5月份
  - No: 五月／六月十九日
  - Yes: 下午3时／上午11时30分
  - No: 两点半／四时十五分
 • Days of the week:
  - Yes: 周一/周二.../周日
  - No: 一/二.../日
3.6.2. Numbers
 • Use half-width Arabic numerals for all numbers, including year, date, and time.
 • Do NOT use separators in numbers (e.g., 6000人 not 6,000人).
3.6.3. Currencies
 • Place currency names after the amount.
  - Example: 100欧元 / 150美元
 • Use standardized terms for different currencies.
3.6.4. Units of Measure
 • Always convert to metric units (e.g., from inch to meter/cm).
3.6.5. Numeral Exception
 • Use Chinese characters ONLY for quantifiers.
  - Yes: 一种旅行方式
  - No: 1次出游
4. Translation Guidelines per Content Type
4.1. User Interface (UI)
 • Applies to banners, buttons, and menu titles.
 • Translations must be clear, short, and efficient due to character limits.
 • Do NOT use punctuation in buttons and menus.
4.2. Call to Actions (CTAs) and Buttons
 • Keep text short and action-oriented.
 • Do NOT add punctuation, including exclamation points.
4.3. Marketing
 • Consider the pace and rhythm of Simplified Chinese.
 • Use locally relevant, conversational terms.
 • Write casually, authentically, and directly, as if speaking to a good friend.
4.4. Legal, Policies, Terms & Conditions
 • Prioritize accuracy and faithfulness to the source text.
 • Do NOT deviate from the English text.
 • Do NOT omit or add content.
 • Do NOT merge or split sentences.
 • Ensure accuracy and naturalness, especially for numbers and dates.
