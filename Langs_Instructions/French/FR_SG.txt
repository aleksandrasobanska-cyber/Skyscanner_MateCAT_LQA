# Skyscanner French (FR) – LQA Calibration & Style Guide

## 1. Style Guide Summary

### 1.1. Voice & Tone
- **Creative license** — transcreate freely; don't follow English word-for-word
- Text should sound as if **written directly in French**
- Message must remain intact, but phrasing can change completely
- Use simple, accessible language — speak like everyday people
- Brand name: **Skyscanner** (keep in English)

### 1.2. Form of Address
| Form | Usage |
|------|-------|
| **vous** | Always use (formal) |
| **tu** | **NEVER use** — even in casual contexts |

### 1.3. Punctuation & Capitalisation

#### Titles
| Rule | ✓ Correct | ✗ Incorrect |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| No English-style title case | Vous pouvez compter sur nous | Vous Pouvez Compter Sur Nous |
| No full stop (single sentence) | Vous pouvez compter sur nous | Vous pouvez compter sur nous. |
| Full stop OK (multi-sentence) | Besoin d'un hôtel ? Nous pouvons vous aider. | Besoin d'un hôtel ? Nous pouvons vous aider |

#### Bullet Lists
| List Type | Format |
|-----------|--------|
| Phrases/sentences | Full stop after each item |
| Single words/concepts | No full stops |
| **Never use** | Semicolons (;) with final full stop |

### 1.4. Dashes & Hyphens
- **Avoid en-dash (–)** for expressing ":" — use colon or new sentence instead
- When splitting into sentences, ensure both have proper punctuation

| ✓ Correct | ✗ Incorrect |
|-----------|-------------|
| New York vous attend : réservez dès maintenant. | New York vous attend – réservez dès maintenant. |
| New York vous attend. Réservez dès maintenant. | New York vous attend. Réservez dès maintenant |

### 1.5. Special Characters & Formatting
| Element | Rule | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Accents on capitals | Always use | Évadez-vous ✓ (not Evadez-vous) |
| Ampersand (&) | Never use | "hôtel et vol" ✓ (not "hôtel & vol") |
| "and/or" | Use "ou" only | "ou" ✓ (not "et/ou") |
| Guillemets | French style with spaces | « texte » |
| Apostrophes | Straight | l'hôtel (not l'hôtel with curved) |
| Ordinals | No superscripts | 18e, 1er ✓ (not XVIIIᵉ, 1ᵉʳ) |

### 1.6. Non-breaking Spaces
Required in French typography (shown with _):

| Context | Format |
|---------|--------|
| Dates | 18_mars_2020 |
| Before ? ! : ; | Des questions_? |
| Numbers + labels | Question_3 |
| Time | 20_h_59 |
| After symbols | *_Données |

### 1.7. Numbers, Dates & Currency
| Element | Format | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Decimal separator | Comma | 1 000,50 |
| Thousand separator | Space | 1 000 000 |
| Date format | DD Month YYYY | 18 mars 2024 |
| Time | 24h with spaces | 8 h 20 |
| Currency | After amount + nbsp | 99 € |

### 1.8. Buttons/CTAs/Clickable Links
- Use **infinitive form**
- Use **"me/I" voice** instead of "you"

| ✓ Correct | ✗ Incorrect |
|-----------|-------------|
| Réserver mon vol | Réservez votre vol |
| Consulter mes réservations | Consultez vos réservations |

### 1.9. Anglicisms
- **Don't avoid at all costs** — use common French anglicisms
- Avoid jargon/obscure English terms
- Prefer French when commonly used: "enregistrement" over "check-in"
- OK to use: "marketing", "email" (if commonly used in French)

### 1.10. Accessibility & Inclusion
- **Avoid gendered adjectives** when referring to users
- Rephrase to be neutral; use "(e)" only as last resort

| ✓ Preferred | ✗ Avoid |
|-------------|---------|
| Vous recevrez votre remboursement. | Vous serez remboursé(e). |
| Est-ce que … vous apporte satisfaction ? | Êtes-vous satisfait(e) de… ? |

### 1.11. Repetitions
- **Avoid repetitions** — English is more liberal with them
- Use pronouns, synonyms, or restructure

| ✓ Correct | ✗ Incorrect |
|-----------|-------------|
| Vous souhaitez réserver un vol ? Trouvez les meilleurs prix et faites-le dès aujourd'hui. | Vous souhaitez réserver un vol ? Trouvez les meilleurs prix et réservez un vol aujourd'hui. |

### 1.12. Plurals
- **Avoid "(s)"** — rephrase or pick one form
- Use only as last resort when context makes meaning clear

## 2. LQA CALIBRATION: FALSE POSITIVE PREVENTION

**CRITICAL:** This section defines what the evaluator must **ACCEPT**. Flagging these creates unnecessary validation burden.

---

### 2.1. Creative Translation Tolerance

The Style Guide explicitly grants **creative license** for transcreation.

#### 2.1.1. Do NOT Flag as "Mistranslation"
- Sentences completely restructured for natural French
- English structure abandoned for idiomatic French
- Phrasing that sounds "written directly in French"
- Different word choices that preserve meaning
- Repetitions avoided through pronouns or restructuring

#### 2.1.2. Do NOT Flag as "Omission/Addition"
- Elements restructured for French flow
- Repetitive terms replaced with pronouns
- "and/or" translated as just "ou"
- Context-appropriate adaptations

**Only flag** if **core meaning is changed, lost, or contradicted**.

---

### 2.2. Form of Address Tolerance

#### 2.2.1. Do NOT Flag
- Consistent use of "vous" throughout
- "Vous" in all contexts (casual or formal)

#### 2.2.2. Only Flag If
- "Tu" used instead of "vous" (NEVER acceptable)
- Inconsistent mixing of forms

---

### 2.3. Punctuation & Capitalisation Tolerance

#### 2.3.1. Titles
**Do NOT Flag:**
- Sentence case (not English title case)
- Single-sentence titles without full stop
- Multi-sentence titles with full stops

**Only Flag If:**
- English-style title case applied
- Inconsistent punctuation in multi-sentence titles

#### 2.3.2. Bullet Lists
**Do NOT Flag:**
- Full stops after phrase items
- No punctuation for single-word items
- Consistent formatting within list

**Only Flag If:**
- Semicolons used with final full stop (old French style)
- Inconsistent punctuation within same list

#### 2.3.3. Dashes
**Do NOT Flag:**
- Colons used instead of en-dashes
- New sentences instead of dashes
- Proper punctuation on both parts

**Only Flag If:**
- En-dash creates confusion
- Incomplete punctuation when sentences are split

---

### 2.4. Non-breaking Space Tolerance

#### 2.4.1. Do NOT Flag
- Non-breaking spaces before : ; ! ? (often not visually verifiable)
- Non-breaking spaces in dates, times
- Regular spaces in contexts where non-breaking would be ideal but display is correct
- Minor spacing variations

#### 2.4.2. Only Flag If
- Missing space entirely before double punctuation
- Line breaks within date/time units (if verifiable)
- Spacing creates clear visual issues

---

### 2.5. Special Character Tolerance

#### 2.5.1. Do NOT Flag
- Accents on capital letters (É, À, etc.)
- Straight apostrophes (common on French keyboards)
- Regular ordinals without superscripts (18e, 1er)
- French guillemets « » with spaces

#### 2.5.2. Only Flag If
- Missing accents on capitals where required
- Ampersand (&) used instead of "et"
- "et/ou" used instead of "ou"
- Superscripts causing display issues

---

### 2.6. Button/CTA Voice Tolerance

#### 2.6.1. Do NOT Flag
- Infinitive form: "Réserver mon vol"
- "Me/I" voice: "mon vol", "mes réservations"
- Consistent CTA style throughout

#### 2.6.2. Only Flag If
- Imperative "vous" form in CTAs: "Réservez votre vol"
- Inconsistent mixing of styles in same context

---

### 2.7. Anglicism Tolerance

#### 2.7.1. Do NOT Flag
- Common French anglicisms used naturally (marketing, email)
- French equivalents used for terms with good French alternatives
- Consistent terminology choices

#### 2.7.2. Only Flag If
- Obscure English jargon that French users wouldn't understand
- Anglicism used when clear French term is more common
- Inconsistent use of same term

---

### 2.8. Gender Neutrality Tolerance

#### 2.8.1. Do NOT Flag
- Sentences restructured to avoid gendered adjectives
- Neutral formulations used
- "(e)" used only when unavoidable
- Consistent approach to gender throughout

#### 2.8.2. Only Flag If
- Unnecessarily gendered when neutral option exists
- Inconsistent gender handling

---

### 2.9. Number & Date Format Tolerance

#### 2.9.1. Do NOT Flag
- Comma for decimals, space for thousands
- Date format: DD Month YYYY
- 24h time format with spaces: 8 h 20
- Currency after amount: 99 €

#### 2.9.2. Only Flag If
- Wrong decimal separator (period instead of comma)
- Wrong date order
- Currency format creates confusion

---

### 2.10. Terminology & Synonym Tolerance

| English | Acceptable French Options |
|---------|---------------------------|
| Flight | vol |
| Book | réserver |
| Search | rechercher, chercher |
| Deal / Offer | offre, promotion |
| Trip | voyage, séjour |
| Hotel | hôtel |
| Reload/Refresh | Recharger (preferred over Rafraîchir) |
| Read (documents) | Consulter (preferred), Lire (acceptable) |
| Check-in | enregistrement (preferred) |
| Save | économiser (money), enregistrer (data) |

**Do NOT flag** reasonable synonym choices that preserve meaning.

---

### 2.11. Repetition Avoidance Tolerance

#### 2.11.1. Do NOT Flag
- Pronouns used to avoid repeating nouns
- Synonyms used for variety
- Restructured sentences that eliminate repetition
- "faites-le" instead of repeating "réservez un vol"

#### 2.11.2. Only Flag If
- Pronoun reference is unclear
- Meaning is lost due to changes

---

### 2.12. Locale Tolerance

#### 2.12.1. Do NOT Flag
- Terms kept inclusive for all French-speaking regions
- Not over-localized for France only
- Standards that work internationally

#### 2.12.2. Only Flag If
- France-specific terms exclude other French speakers
- Regional terms that cause confusion

---

### 2.13. Severity Calibration

| Severity | Apply When |
|----------|------------|
| **Critical** | Placeholder corruption, complete meaning inversion, broken tags, functional errors |
| **Major** | Core meaning changed, "tu" used instead of "vous", factual errors, ampersand used |
| **Minor** | Style preferences within acceptable range, minor formatting inconsistencies |
| **Do Not Flag** | Issues covered by tolerance rules 2.1–2.12 |

---

### 2.14. Pre-Flag Checklist

Before flagging, verify:

1. ☐ Is this a **creative transcreation** that sounds natural in French?
2. ☐ Is **"vous"** used consistently (not "tu")?
3. ☐ Does the punctuation follow **French rules** (not English)?
4. ☐ Is repetition **avoided** through pronouns or restructuring?
5. ☐ Does the Style Guide **explicitly prohibit** this choice?
6. ☐ Would a native French speaker find this **incorrect** (not just different from source)?

**If meaning is preserved AND the translation sounds naturally French → DO NOT FLAG**
