# Skyscanner Hindi (HI-IN) – LQA Calibration & Style Guide

## 1. Skyscanner Voice & Mission

### 1.1. Brand Promise
Skyscanner aims to be the traveller's helpful, honest ally. Our writing:
- Starts with empathy and understanding
- Provides clear information that inspires action
- Offers helpful tips that make travel planning simple, easy, and delightful
- Gives honest, transparent advice

### 1.2. Core Writing Principles
- **Traveller First:** Always put the traveller at the center of the copy
- **Be Helpful:** Every sentence should provide value to the user
- **Be Conversational:** Write as if talking to a friend
- **Be Simple:** Choose simple words and short sentences
- **Be Specific:** Provide concrete information users can act on
- **Be Honest:** Help users make informed decisions
- **Avoid Self-Promotion:** Focus on user benefits, not company features

**Important:** Skyscanner does NOT offer discounts — discounts are offered by providers (e.g., Trip.com). Never imply Skyscanner is the source of discounts.

## 2. Language-Specific Guidelines

### 2.1. Spelling Rules

#### Verb Forms
Use ए and ई spellings:
- ✅ गए, लिए, गई, हुई, हुए
- ❌ *गये, *लिये, *गयी, *हुयी, *हुये

#### Anuswar (ं) Usage
Use anuswar instead of writing पंचम वर्ण in half:
- ✅ संपर्क, इंटरनेट, कंप्यूटर, इंडिया, दिसंबर
- ❌ सम्पर्क, इन्टरनेट, कम्प्यूटर, इन्डिया, दिसम्बर

#### Chandrabindu (ँ)
Use chandrabindu for plurality and nasalization:
- Plurality: लड़कियाँ
- Nasalization: हँसना, पाँच, बाँटना, ढूँढ़ना, जाएँगे

**Note:** Do not replace with anuswar — हँस (laugh) vs हंस (swan) have different meanings.

#### Nuqta Usage
Use nuqta with ज़ and फ़ only (not with क, ख, ग):
- ✅ ज़रूरी, फ़ोन, फ़्लाइट
- ❌ क़, ख़, ग़

#### Preferred Spellings
| Preferred | Avoid |
|-----------|-------|
| ढूँढना | ढूँढ़ना |
| दोबारा | दुबारा |
| दोपहर | दुपहर |
| अकसर | अक्सर |
| बिलकुल | बिल्कुल |

### 2.2. Punctuation

- Follow standard Hindi punctuation rules
- Space **after** punctuation, not before
- Use Khadi Pai (।) as end punctuation for complete sentences
- **UI Strings:** Match source punctuation — if source has no end punctuation, target should not have one either

### 2.3. Addressing Users

- Always use honorific form **आप** (not तुम or तू)
- Tone: Conversational, respectful, not formal or stiff
- Avoid slang and jargon
- Use gender-neutral greetings: "XXX जी, आपका स्वागत है।"

### 2.4. Gender of Loan Words

| Gender | Words |
|--------|-------|
| **Masculine** | Folder, Audio, Browser, Brand, Button, Calendar, Call, Payment, Click, Connection, Credit, Data, Email, Label, Link, Mobile, Video, Network, Media, Password, App, Computer, Transaction, Access, Template, Process, Device |
| **Feminine** | Photo, File, Billing, Report, Post, Rank, Screen, Team, Library, Profile, Site, Cookies, Feed, Insight |

### 2.5. Active Voice
- Prefer active voice for directness
- Passive voice acceptable when:
  - Agent is unimportant or unknown
  - Authoritative tone is needed (legal/T&C content)

### 2.6. Pluralization of Transliterated Words

- **Do NOT** apply Hindi pluralization to English words
  - ❌ कंप्यूटरों
- **Can** transliterate English plural forms when necessary
  - ✅ फ़्लाइट डील्स (for "flight deals")
- **Can** use quantifiers (कुछ, कई, अनेक, सभी) to indicate plurality
  - ✅ कुछ ही टैप में (for "in a few taps")
- **Accepted adapted plurals:** फ़ाइलें, साइटें, वेबसाइटें, कंपनियाँ, डिवाइसों

### 2.7. Numbers & Dates

#### Numbers
- Use international numerals (1, 2, 3), not Devanagari (१, २, ३)
- Convert millions to lakhs/crores:
  - 1 million = 10 लाख
  - 10 million = 1 करोड़
  - 1 billion = 100 करोड़ / 1 अरब
- Use comma as thousand separator (Indian system):
  - ₹1,000 | ₹10,000 | ₹1,00,000 | ₹10,00,000
- Ranges: Use hyphen without spaces (2-4 यात्री)

#### Dates
- Format: dd-mm-yyyy
- Full form: 12 जून, 2023 (comma after month)

#### Time
- UI strings: 24-hour format
- Other content: Contextual (सुबह 8 बजे, शाम 9 बजे)
- With dynamic variables: Leave AM/PM in English

#### Abbreviations
| Full | Abbreviation |
|------|--------------|
| घंटा/घंटे | घं. |
| मिनट | मि. |
| सेकंड | से. |
| सोमवार | सोम |
| मंगलवार | मंगल |
| बुधवार | बुध |
| गुरुवार | गुरु |
| शुक्रवार | शुक्र |
| शनिवार | शनि |
| रविवार | रवि |

### 2.8. Currencies & Units
- Keep currencies as in source (do not convert)
- Symbol left of number, no space: ₹30, $30
- Indian currency: Use ₹ symbol; expansion: रुपया/रुपये

### 2.9. Company & Place Names

#### Company Names
- **Skyscanner:** Always keep in English
- **Third-party companies:** Keep in English
- **International organizations:** Translate if official Hindi version exists (e.g., अंतरराष्ट्रीय मुद्रा कोष)
- **Difficult foreign names:** Leave in English (e.g., Château de Linardié)
- **Acronyms:** Leave in English; well-known ones in Hindi (यूनेस्को, सिम, पिन)

#### Place Names
- Cities/Countries: Transliterate in popular form (कनाडा, पेरिस, टेक्सास)
- Airport names: Do NOT add "airport" unless in source
- POIs: Transliterate

#### URLs
- Leave in English
- Rules: No spaces, lowercase only, no special characters, Latin characters only

### 2.10. Content Type Guidelines

| Content Type | Guidelines |
|--------------|------------|
| **UI (Buttons/Commands)** | Use verb form (होटल खोजें) |
| **UI (Menus/Dropdowns)** | Use noun form (सहायता) |
| **Marketing** | Creative adaptation encouraged; translate message, not words |
| **Legal/T&C** | Accurate, formal, stick close to source |

## 3. LQA CALIBRATION: FALSE POSITIVE PREVENTION GUIDELINES

**CRITICAL INSTRUCTION:** This section defines patterns that the LQA Evaluator must **ACCEPT as valid translations**. These represent acceptable variations, natural localization choices, or stylistic decisions that do NOT constitute errors. Flagging issues covered by these rules creates unnecessary reviewer burden.

---

### 3.1. Meaning-Based Translation Tolerance

Translation quality is measured by **meaning preservation and naturalness**, not literal word matching.

#### 3.1.1. Do NOT Flag as "Mistranslation"
- **Restructured sentences** that preserve meaning but use different word order
- **Contextual word choices** where the translator selected a synonym appropriate for Hindi
- **Implied subjects/objects** that are clear from context in Hindi but explicit in English
- **Idiomatic adaptations** that convey the same message in a culturally appropriate way

#### 3.1.2. Do NOT Flag as "Omission"
- **Pronouns** (you, your, I, we) that are implicit in Hindi verb conjugation or context
- **Articles** (a, an, the) which do not exist in Hindi
- **Redundant words** that would sound unnatural if translated literally
- **Filler words** that add no semantic value

#### 3.1.3. Do NOT Flag as "Addition"
- **Postpositions** required by Hindi grammar (को, में, से, पर, के लिए, etc.)
- **Honorific particles** (जी) when addressing users
- **Contextual clarifiers** that make the Hindi sentence grammatically complete
- **Classifiers or particles** required for natural Hindi expression

---

### 3.2. Synonym & Terminology Tolerance

Hindi often has multiple valid translations for English terms. Accept reasonable variations.

#### 3.2.1. Do NOT Flag as "Wrong Term" or "Inconsistency"
When both options are semantically correct and contextually appropriate:

| English | All Acceptable Hindi Options |
|---------|------------------------------|
| Search | खोजें, खोजना, तलाशें, ढूँढें |
| Book | बुक करें, बुकिंग करें |
| Flight | फ़्लाइट, उड़ान, हवाई यात्रा |
| Hotel | होटल |
| Trip | ट्रिप, यात्रा, सफ़र |
| Deal | डील, ऑफ़र, सौदा |
| Save | सेव करें, बचाएँ (context-dependent) |
| Check | देखें, चेक करें |
| Browse | देखें, खोजें, ब्राउज़ करें |
| Get | पाएँ, प्राप्त करें, मिलेगा |

#### 3.2.2. Only Flag Terminology If
- The term **contradicts explicit Termbase entries** AND causes meaning change
- The term creates **factual inaccuracy**
- A **product/feature name** is mistranslated (e.g., "Explore" feature)

---

### 3.3. Transliteration Tolerance

Hindi uses a mix of transliterated English and native Hindi based on common usage.

#### 3.3.1. Do NOT Flag
- Common tech terms in transliterated form (ऐप, ब्राउज़र, डेटा, ईमेल, etc.)
- Brand names kept in English
- Airline/company names in English or transliterated form
- Place names transliterated according to popular usage

#### 3.3.2. Only Flag If
- Transliteration violates the nuqta/anuswar rules in Section 2.1
- A well-known official Hindi term exists but wasn't used
- The transliteration is misspelled or unrecognizable

---

### 3.4. Punctuation Tolerance

#### 3.4.1. Do NOT Flag
- Use of Khadi Pai (।) OR full stop (.) for end punctuation — both are acceptable
- Minor spacing variations around punctuation
- Presence or absence of end punctuation in UI strings (follow source)

#### 3.4.2. Only Flag If
- Punctuation **changes the meaning** (e.g., question mark removed from a question)
- Punctuation is **clearly incorrect** (e.g., multiple consecutive punctuation marks)

---

### 3.5. Formality & Tone Tolerance

The Style Guide prefers conversational tone but accepts variation within respectful bounds.

#### 3.5.1. Do NOT Flag
- Minor variations in formality level that don't affect respect (आप form maintained)
- Choice between Sanskrit-derived and Perso-Arabic-derived synonyms
- Stylistic word order variations

#### 3.5.2. Only Flag If
- **Disrespectful** forms are used (तुम/तू instead of आप)
- Tone is **grossly inappropriate** for context (slang in legal text)
- The tone **actively contradicts** Style Guide requirements for specific content types

---

### 3.6. Spelling Variation Tolerance

Hindi has accepted spelling variations for many words.

#### 3.6.1. Do NOT Flag as "Spelling Error"
Unless the variation:
- Creates **ambiguity** or **meaning change**
- Violates the **specific preferred spellings** listed in Section 2.1
- Is clearly a **typo** (missing characters, wrong characters)

---

### 3.7. Number & Date Format Tolerance

#### 3.7.1. Do NOT Flag
- Minor variations in date separators (-, /, or space)
- Contextual time expressions (सुबह 8 बजे vs 8:00 AM)
- Slight variations in number formatting

#### 3.7.2. Only Flag If
- **Wrong number system** used (Devanagari instead of international)
- **Incorrect conversion** of millions to lakhs/crores
- **Date/time creates confusion** or is factually wrong

---

### 3.8. Gender Agreement Tolerance

#### 3.8.1. Do NOT Flag
- Gender assignment for loan words not listed in Section 2.4 — these can vary
- Contextual gender choices that are grammatically correct

#### 3.8.2. Only Flag If
- Gender assignment **contradicts the list** in Section 2.4
- Gender disagreement creates a **grammatical error** that a native speaker would notice
