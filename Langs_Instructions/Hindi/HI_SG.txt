Skyscanner Hindi LQA Model Instructions

1. Brand Mission and Tone
1.1. Mission
 • All content must reflect Skyscanner’s brand promise: to be the traveller’s helpful, honest ally.
 • Write with empathy and understanding, focusing on the traveller’s needs and journey.
 • Provide clear, actionable information, helpful tips, and honest, transparent advice.
1.2. Tone of Voice
 • Follow Skyscanner’s Tone of Voice as referenced in the linked document (if available).
 • Always prioritize the traveller’s perspective and benefit.

2. General Writing & Translation Guidelines
2.1. Good Writing Principles
 • Always put the traveller first.
 • Avoid excessive adjectives; focus on helpfulness in every sentence.
 • Center the traveller in the copy, emphasizing user benefit and advice.
Examples:
 • ✔ “चाहे रोमांचक यात्रा हो या रुकने के लिए आरामदायक जगह, हर चीज़ पर डील पाएँ।”
 • ✖ “हम दुनिया के बेहतरीन ट्रैवल सर्च इंजन में से एक हैं।”
2.2. Writing & Translation Guidelines
 • Conversational Style: Translations should sound natural and spoken, not formal or stiff.
 • Clarity: Use the most well-known terms; translate meaning, not just words.
  - ✔ “आपका डेटा। आपकी पसंद।”
  - ✖ “आपका डेटा। आपका चुनाव।”
 • Simplicity: Use simple words and short sentences for inclusivity and accessibility.
 • Specificity: Avoid vagueness; provide concrete, actionable information.
  - Vague: “ट्रेन स्टेशन से होटल बहुत ही कम दूरी पर है”
  - Specific: “ट्रेन स्टेशन से होटल की दूरी 1 किमी है”
 • Naturalness: Write as if speaking to a friend; avoid trying to sound overly clever.
 • Interest: Make travel content engaging and lively.
 • Honesty: Be transparent about pros and cons to help users make informed choices.
 • Accuracy: Double-check all factual details (spellings, times, locations, etc.).
 • Exclamation Marks: Use sparingly.
2.2.1. Discounts
 • Do not imply Skyscanner offers discounts; attribute them to providers.
  - ✔ “कुछ चुनिंदा होटल पर 10% डिस्काउंट का ऑफ़र है।”
  - ✖ “चुने हुए कुछ होटल पर हम 10% डिस्काउंट का ऑफ़र दे रहे हैं।”
2.4. Accessibility
 • For strings marked as "accessibility string," follow source instructions for symbols and number formats (e.g., write “10 percent discount” if source spells it out).

3. Language-Specific Translation Guidelines
3.1. Spelling Rules
 • Use ए and ई spellings in verb forms: गए, लिए, गई, हुई, हुए (not गये, लिये, गयी, हुयी, हुये).
 • Use anuswar (ं) instead of half पंचम वर्ण:
  - संपर्क, इंटरनेट, कंप्यूटर, इंडिया, दिसंबर
 • Use chandrabindu (ँ) for plurality and nasalization:
  - लड़कियाँ, हँसना, पाँच, बाँटना, ढूँढ़ना, जाएँगे
 • Do not replace chandrabindu with anusvar.
 • Use nuqta with ‘ज’ and ‘फ’ for foreign terms; do not use with क, ख, ग.
 • Preferred spellings (use consistently):
  - ढूँढना, दोबारा, दोपहर, अकसर, बिलकुल
3.2. Punctuation
 • Follow standard Hindi punctuation.
 • Always add a space after punctuation, not before.
 • Use Khadi Pai (।) as end punctuation.
3.2.1. UI Translation Punctuation
 • Match source punctuation style in UI; do not add end punctuation if not present in source.
Example:
 • Source: Book cheap flights, hotels and car hire with Skyscanner
 • Correct: Skyscanner से किफ़ायती होटल, फ़्लाइट और रेंटल कार बुक करें
 • Incorrect: Skyscanner से किफ़ायती होटल, फ़्लाइट और रेंटल कार बुक करें।
3.3. Addressing Users – YOU
 • Use less formal, conversational, but respectful tone.
 • Avoid slang and jargon.
 • Always use honorific form ‘Aap’ (आप) and corresponding verb forms.
3.4. Gender of Loan Words
 • Assign gender to all nouns, including loan words.
 • Masculine: Folder, Audio, Browser, Brand, Button, Calendar, Call, Payment, Click, Connection, Credit, Data, Email, Label, Link, Mobile, Video, Network, Media, Password, App, Computer, Transaction, Access, Template, Process, Device
 • Feminine: Photo, File, Billing, Report, Post, Rank, Screen, Team, Library, Profile, Site, Cookies, Feed, Insight
3.4.1. Gender-Neutral Language
 • Prioritize gender neutrality.
 • Use neutral greetings for placeholders (e.g., “XXX जी, आपका स्वागत है।”).
 • Example:
  - Source: When can I contact you?
  - Correct: आपसे संपर्क करने का सही समय क्या रहेगा?
  - Incorrect: मैं आपसे किस समय संपर्क कर सकता हूँ?
3.5. Direct Communication with Users
 • Use a neutral, empathetic, and helpful tone.
 • Example:
  - Source: “Hello, Manish! Good morning. Please tell me your issue. I will be happy to help you.”
  - Correct: “हैलो, मनीष! गुड मॉर्निंग। कृपया अपनी समस्या मुझे बताएँ। आपकी मदद करके मुझे खुशी होगी।”
  - Incorrect: “नमस्ते, मनीष! सुप्रभात। कृपया अपनी समस्या के बारे में मुझे बताएँ। मैं आपकी मदद करके खुश होऊँगा।”
 • For form field and error messages:
  - Keep copy short and succinct.
  - Flag the error first, then add instructions.
  - Use passive voice to avoid blame.
  - Example: “यह नाम रिकॉर्ड में मौजूद नहीं है। कृपया फिर से कोशिश करें।”
  - Error messaging: Be polite, impersonal, supportive, and non-accusatory.
  - Always state the problem and solution.
  - Use plain language and active voice.
  - Use a friendly tone; never blame travellers.
 • For success messages: Be mindful of user context (e.g., not all travel is for leisure).
3.6. Active Voice
 • Prefer active voice for directness and effectiveness.
 • Example:
  - Source: By using this coupon within 24 hours, you will get one more for free.
  - Correct: 24 घंटों के अंदर इस कूपन का इस्तेमाल करने पर आपको एक और फ़्री कूपन मिलेगा।
  - Incorrect: अगर आप इस कूपन का इस्तेमाल 24 घंटों के अंदर करेंगे, तभी आपको एक और एक और फ़्री कूपन मिलेगा।
 • Use passive voice only when:
  - The agent is unimportant or unknown.
  - An authoritative tone is needed (e.g., terms and conditions).
3.7. Pluralization of Transliterated Words
 • Do not pluralize English words using Hindi rules (e.g., कंप्यूटरों is incorrect).
 • Transliterate English words in plural form only if necessary to convey plural sense.
 • Use quantifiers (कुछ, कई, अनेक, सभी) for plural forms instead of transliterating plurals.
 • Commonly accepted plural transliterations: फ़ाइलें/फ़ाइलों, साइटें/साइटों, वेबसाइटें/वेबसाइटों, कंपनियाँ/कंपनियों, डिवाइसों.
3.8. Formats
3.8.1. Numbers, Dates, and Time
 • Use international numerals (1, 2, 3, …), not Devanagari (१, २, ३, …).
 • Use सौ, हज़ार, लाख, करोड़, अरब for large numbers.
 • Convert millions to lakhs/crores as per table:
  - 1 million = 10 लाख
  - 10 million = 1 करोड़
  - 100 million = 10 करोड़
  - 250 million = 25 करोड़
  - 1 billion = 100 करोड़/1 अरब
  - 10 billion = 1000 करोड़
  - 100 billion = 10,000 करोड़
 • Use comma as thousand separator for numbers >3 digits.
  - ₹10, ₹100, ₹1,000, ₹10,000, ₹1,00,000, ₹10,00,000
 • Use hyphen for ranges: 2-4 यात्री
 • Dates: dd-mm-yyyy format; full form as 12 जून, 2023 (comma after month).
 • Time: 24-hour format for UI; AM/PM in Hindi for other content (e.g., सुबह 8 बजे, रात 9 बजे).
 • For variable time values, leave AM/PM in English.
 • Abbreviations:
  - घंटा/घंटे = घं.
  - मिनट = मि.
  - सेकंड = से.
  - सोमवार = सोम, मंगलवार = मंगल, बुधवार = बुध, गुरुवार = गुरु, शुक्रवार = शुक्र, शनिवार = शनि, रविवार = रवि
3.8.2. Currencies and Units
 • Write currencies as in the source unless instructed otherwise.
 • Currency symbol to the left of the number, no space: $30, ₹30
 • Use ₹ for Indian currency; in expansion, use रुपया or रुपये.
 • Apply same rule to units of measurement.
3.8.3. Countries, Cities, Suppliers
 • Use placeholders for place names; refer to context notes for type (airport, city, etc.).
 • For marketing, transliterate country/city names; leave airport names in English if not commonly used in Hindi.
 • Same rule for first name/company/supplier tags.
3.8.4. URL Translation
 • Leave URLs in English.
 • URL formatting rules:
1. No spaces; use hyphens only.
2. All lowercase.
3. No extended/special characters.
4. Only Latin characters.
 • Use http://www.url-encode-decode.com/ for validation if needed.
3.9. Linguistic Sequence
3.9.1. Company Names and Third-Party Products
 • Leave company and product names in English; follow English capitalization.
 • Translate international organization/NGO/ministry names if Hindi version exists; otherwise, transliterate.
 • Leave hard-to-pronounce foreign names in English.
 • Acronyms/abbreviations: leave in English if unfamiliar; otherwise, spell out and provide short form in parentheses.
 • Well-known acronyms in Hindi: write in Hindi (e.g., सिम, पिन).
3.9.2. Geo-Terms
 • Airport names: do not add "airport" when translating toponyms.
 • City/country names: transliterate in popular form.
  - Examples:
   - Canada: कनाडा (not कैनेडा)
   - Israel: इज़राइल (not इज़रीयल)
   - Texas: टेक्सास (not टेक्सस)
   - Paris: पेरिस (not पैरिस)
 • Points of Interest: transliterate.
 • Use the most commonly used and understandable version.
3.9.3. Tax/Legal Terms
 • Flag any tax/legal term that may not be relevant to the market.
Localization
 • Ensure translations sound native, are easily understandable, nuanced, crisp, colloquial, and culturally appropriate.
 • Adapt contextually (e.g., use पेट्रोल पंप for “gas station” in India).

4. Translation Guidelines per Content Type
User Interface (UI)
 • Pay attention to context and character limits.
 • Use verb forms for buttons/commands; noun forms for menus/dropdowns.
 • For options/checkboxes, decide based on context.
Examples:
 • Search hotels: होटल खोजें (not होटल खोजना)
 • Log in: लॉग इन करें (not लॉग इन)
 • Help: सहायता (not सहायता करें)
Marketing and Marketing Emails
 • Focus on key message, not literal translation.
 • Use creativity to make content natural and appealing in Hindi.
Legal, Policies, Terms & Conditions
 • Be accurate and clear; stick closely to the source.
 • Use formal tone and address.
