# Skyscanner Swedish (SV) – LQA Calibration & Style Guide

## 1. Style Guide Summary

### 1.1. Voice & Tone
- Use **Swedish** — natural, friendly, conversational
- Address users with **informal singular "du"** (not formal/plural "ni")
- Write as if talking to a friend — clear, direct, approachable
- **Transcreation encouraged:** Change word order and structure to sound natural Swedish
- **Consistency is key:** Use same vocabulary for similar actions
- **Clarity:** Front-load information, be concise and efficient
- Skyscanner = **Skyscanner** (do not translate)

### 1.2. Active Voice
- **Always prefer active voice** — stronger and more direct
- **Avoid passive voice** — sounds impersonal
  - ✓ "Vi skickar dig en bekräftelse."
  - ✗ "En bekräftelse kommer att skickas till dig."

### 1.3. Language & Terminology
- **Avoid jargon** — use terminology people are familiar with
- **Avoid uncommon abbreviations**
- Follow **Svenska skrivregler** (Språkrådet) for general grammar

### 1.4. Punctuation

| Mark | Swedish Usage |
|------|---------------|
| Comma (,) | Follow Swedish rules; differs from English in some contexts |
| Period (.) | Standard sentence ending |
| Quotation marks | Swedish style: "citattecken" or »citattecken« |
| Colon (:) | Lowercase after colon (unless proper noun) |
| Exclamation (!) | Use sparingly |

### 1.5. Numbers & Dates

| Context | Swedish Format |
|---------|----------------|
| Thousands separator | Space (1 000, 10 000) |
| Decimal separator | Comma (3,5) |
| Date format | YYYY-MM-DD or D månad YYYY (15 januari 2024) |
| Time format | 24-hour (14:30, 09.00) |
| Percentages | Space before %: 50 % |

### 1.6. Currencies
- **Swedish kronor:** SEK or kr
- Format: 100 kr or 100 SEK (after number)
- Space between number and currency

### 1.7. Compound Words
- Swedish typically writes compounds as **one word**
  - ✓ "flygbokning" (flight booking)
  - ✓ "prisövervakning" (price alert)
  - ✗ "flyg bokning" (two words)

### 1.8. Capitalization
- **Lowercase** for months, days, nationalities, languages
  - ✓ "januari", "måndag", "svensk", "svenska"
  - ✗ "Januari", "Måndag", "Svensk"
- **No title case** — don't capitalize every word in headings

### 1.9. Names & Toponyms
- **Skyscanner:** Keep in English (never translate)
- **Brand names:** Keep in original language
- **City/country names:** Use established Swedish names where they exist
  - ✓ "Köpenhamn" (Copenhagen), "Tyskland" (Germany)

## 2. LQA CALIBRATION: FALSE POSITIVE PREVENTION

**CRITICAL:** This section defines what the evaluator must **ACCEPT**. Flagging these creates unnecessary validation burden.

---

### 2.1. Informal Address ("Du") Tolerance

Swedish uses informal "du" for direct, friendly communication.

#### 2.1.1. Acceptable Address Forms

| Form | Accept? |
|------|---------|
| "du" (singular informal) | ✓ Correct and preferred |
| "ni" (plural/formal) | ✗ Flag unless addressing group |
| Omitted pronoun where natural | ✓ Accept |

**Do NOT flag:**
- Consistent use of "du" throughout
- Natural omission of "du" where understood from context
- "Din/ditt/dina" possessive forms

**Only flag** if "ni" used inappropriately or formality inconsistent.

---

### 2.2. Transcreation Tolerance

The Style Guide explicitly encourages **changing word order** and structure for natural Swedish.

#### 2.2.1. Do NOT Flag as "Mistranslation"
- Restructured sentences that preserve meaning
- Different word order than English source
- Swedish-idiomatic expressions replacing literal translations
- Front-loaded information (key info moved to beginning)
- Condensed/more efficient phrasing

#### 2.2.2. Do NOT Flag as "Omission/Addition"
- Words added for natural Swedish grammar
- Words omitted that are redundant in Swedish
- Restructuring that conveys same message more naturally
- Swedish sentence structure conventions applied

**Only flag** if core meaning is changed or lost.

---

### 2.3. Active Voice Tolerance

#### 2.3.1. Do NOT Flag
- Active voice constructions (preferred)
- Restructuring from passive to active
- Natural Swedish phrasing that avoids passive

#### 2.3.2. Only Flag If
- Passive voice when active would be clearly better and more direct
- Meaning changed by voice transformation

---

### 2.4. Compound Word Tolerance

Swedish compounds are typically written as one word.

#### 2.4.1. Do NOT Flag
- Properly formed compound words (flygresa, hotellbokning)
- Hyphenated compounds where appropriate
- Compounds following Swedish word formation rules

#### 2.4.2. Only Flag If
- Compound incorrectly split into separate words
- Incorrect compound formation
- Missing or incorrect hyphenation affecting meaning

---

### 2.5. Number & Date Format Tolerance

#### 2.5.1. Acceptable Formats

| Element | Acceptable |
|---------|------------|
| Thousands | Space separator (1 000) |
| Decimals | Comma (3,5) |
| Date | YYYY-MM-DD OR D månad YYYY |
| Time | 24-hour (14:30 or 14.30) |
| Percentage | Space before %: 50 % |
| Currency | 100 kr OR 100 SEK |

**Do NOT flag:**
- Either date format if consistent
- Colon or period in time (14:30 or 14.30)
- kr or SEK for Swedish currency

#### 2.5.2. Only Flag If
- Wrong decimal separator (period instead of comma)
- Wrong thousands separator (comma or period)
- Inconsistent date/time format within same text

---

### 2.6. Capitalization Tolerance

Swedish has different capitalization rules than English.

#### 2.6.1. Do NOT Flag
- Lowercase months (januari, februari)
- Lowercase days (måndag, tisdag)
- Lowercase nationalities/languages (svensk, svenska)
- Lowercase after colon (unless proper noun)
- No title case in headings (only first word capitalized)

#### 2.6.2. Only Flag If
- Missing capital at sentence start
- Proper nouns in lowercase
- English-style title case (capitalizing every word)

---

### 2.7. Punctuation Tolerance

#### 2.7.1. Do NOT Flag
- Swedish quotation mark styles ("..." or »...«)
- Different comma placement following Swedish rules
- Removal of exclamation marks for more measured Swedish tone
- Space before percentage (50 %)

#### 2.7.2. Only Flag If
- Question becomes statement
- Punctuation creates meaning confusion
- Clearly incorrect punctuation by Swedish standards

---

### 2.8. Terminology & Synonym Tolerance

| English | Acceptable Swedish Options |
|---------|---------------------------|
| Flight | flyg, flygresa, flygning |
| Book (verb) | boka, reservera |
| Search | sök, söka, leta |
| Deal | erbjudande, fynd |
| Trip | resa, utflykt |
| Hotel | hotell |
| Save (money) | spara |
| Save (bookmark) | spara |
| App | app, applikation |
| Price alert | prisbevakning, prisövervakning |

**Do NOT flag** reasonable synonym choices unless they change meaning.

---

### 2.9. Abbreviation Tolerance

#### 2.9.1. Do NOT Flag
- Common, widely understood abbreviations
- Spelled-out forms instead of abbreviations (preferred)
- Standard Swedish abbreviations (t.ex., m.m., osv.)

#### 2.9.2. Only Flag If
- Uncommon abbreviation that may confuse users
- Inconsistent abbreviation use

---

### 2.10. Gender & Pronoun Tolerance

Swedish has common (en) and neuter (ett) genders.

#### 2.10.1. Do NOT Flag
- Correct gender agreement (en resa, ett flyg)
- "Man" as generic pronoun where natural
- Gender-neutral alternatives where appropriate
- "Du" constructions avoiding gendered language

#### 2.10.2. Only Flag If
- Incorrect grammatical gender (en/ett mismatch)
- Gender agreement errors in adjectives/articles

---

### 2.11. Definite/Indefinite Article Tolerance

Swedish uses suffixed definite articles.

#### 2.11.1. Do NOT Flag
- Correct use of suffixed definite form (resan, flyget, hotellet)
- Freestanding article where grammatically required (den, det, de)
- Natural variation between definite/indefinite based on context

#### 2.11.2. Only Flag If
- Incorrect definite form suffix
- Wrong article affecting meaning

---

### 2.12. Severity Calibration

| Severity | Apply When |
|----------|------------|
| **Critical** | Placeholder corruption, complete meaning inversion, broken tags |
| **Major** | Core meaning changed, factual errors, wrong formality ("ni" instead of "du" throughout) |
| **Minor** | Style preferences, minor inconsistencies within acceptable range |
| **Do Not Flag** | Issues covered by tolerance rules 2.1–2.11 |

---

### 2.13. Pre-Flag Checklist

Before flagging, verify:

1. ☐ Is this a **natural restructuring** for Swedish word order and flow?
2. ☐ Is the **informal "du" address** consistently used?
3. ☐ Is **active voice** preferred where possible?
4. ☐ Are **compound words** correctly formed (one word, not split)?
5. ☐ Does **capitalization** follow Swedish rules (lowercase months, days)?
6. ☐ Is the **core meaning preserved** despite structural changes?
7. ☐ Would a native Swedish speaker find this **incorrect** (not just different)?
8. ☐ Is the translation **clear and concise** as the Style Guide requires?

**If meaning is preserved AND the structure sounds natural in Swedish → DO NOT FLAG**
